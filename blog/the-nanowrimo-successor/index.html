NaNoWriMo Is Gone. Here’s What Comes Next.

July 10, 2025 · By Josh

The NaNoWriMo Successor: The TypeSlate Creator Community

All things come to an end. Some fade out quietly. Others collapse under the weight of their own mismanagement. Unfortunately, NaNoWriMo appears to be the latter. For many writers, it was more than a November challenge. It was a place to set goals, track progress, and connect with fellow creatives.

The recent shutdown of the NaNoWriMo website and the surrounding controversy have left many feeling displaced. That doesn’t mean the core of NaNoWriMo needs to die. Writing is still very much alive.

We’re rebuilding that creative space on stronger ground. The TypeSlate Creator Community is hosted on Substack, a platform made for writers and designed for direct connection.

Here’s what you’ll find in the Creator Community:

  • Writing challenges and word count sprints
  • Weekly posts with prompts, tools, and process discussions
  • Community chat threads and comment sections
  • A shared space for writers who care about staying focused and finishing strong

Anyone can join. You do not need to be a TypeSlate user to subscribe or participate.

What TypeSlate Offers That NaNoWriMo Couldn’t

TypeSlate is a local-first writing app focused on real work and real progress. If the collapse of NaNoWriMo left you thinking more seriously about where your words live, TypeSlate might be exactly what you need.

Here’s how it works:

  • Save your writing to any folder—local, cloud-synced, or external
  • No account login or server dependency
  • Fully offline by design
  • Word tracking and writing stats stored locally on your machine

You are in full control. Even if development paused tomorrow, the software would keep working. That’s the benefit of a tool that respects your data and your process.

A Community Designed to Last

We’re not trying to clone NaNoWriMo. We’re offering an alternative built for independence and resilience. The TypeSlate Creator Community is a place for writers who value honesty, focus, and momentum and don’t depend on any single platform to keep them going.
